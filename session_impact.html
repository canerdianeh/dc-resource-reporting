What I genuinely cannot know
  • ⚠
    Inference hardware efficiency — Anthropic's actual hardware, utilization rate, and power-per-token figure are not publicly disclosed. The 0.001 kWh/1k token estimate is derived from published GPT-4 class analyses and could be 3–5× optimistic on modern inference hardware, or 2× conservative depending on batch size and chip generation.
  • ⚠
    Prompt caching — Anthropic's infrastructure may cache repeated context segments, reducing effective input token processing. If prompt caching was active, actual compute (and therefore energy/water) could be substantially lower than the cumulative context estimate suggests.
  • ⚠
    Data center water use rate — The 1.8 L/kWh figure is the Siddik et al. (2021) median for US evaporative-cooled facilities. Anthropic's data centers may use different cooling systems, and their actual WUE is unknown. The range 0.15–0.79 L for this session reflects that uncertainty.
  • ⚠
    Exact token counts — Claude does not have access to its own token counters. The estimates above are derived from content size heuristics (bytes ÷ bytes/token) and conversation structure, not from instrumented measurement.
  • ⚠
    Scope — These figures cover inference only. They exclude: embodied carbon in hardware manufacturing, network transmission energy, energy for the user's iPad and WiFi router, and indirect water in grid electricity generation. Full lifecycle figures would be meaningfully higher.
The honest bottom line This session consumed approximately the water content of one standard water bottle (best estimate: 0.79 liters, plausible range 0.15–0.79 L) and cost approximately $2.72 in API-equivalent compute. The CO₂ footprint (~0.18 kg) is equivalent to driving about half a mile. These are individually trivial figures. At scale — millions of sessions per day across the industry — they are not.
Methods: Token estimates derived from content heuristics (1 token ≈ 4 bytes for mixed HTML/prose). Energy estimate: 0.001 kWh per 1,000 tokens (Patterson et al. 2021; Luccioni et al. 2023).
