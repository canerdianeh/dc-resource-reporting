US Population Density Quintiles
County-level population density from 2020 Census, grouped into five quintiles. Used to categorize residential water use benchmarks. Hover counties for details.

Population Density by County — 2020 Census (persons/sq mi)

Quintile definitions (persons/sq mi): Q1 Rural <15 · Q2 Semi-rural 15–100 · Q3 Low suburban 100–500 · Q4 Medium urban 500–2,000 · Q5 Dense urban >2,000. Water benchmarks: Rural ~0.9 AF/ac/yr (wells, large lots, irrigation) → Dense urban ~0.6 AF/ac/yr (apartments, no landscaping, efficient municipal supply). The counterintuitive finding: rural residents often use more water per acre than dense urban dwellers due to irrigation, livestock, and less-efficient infrastructure.
